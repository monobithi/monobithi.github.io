About

Hello voyager,

Welcome aboard on the ship — your very own Monobithi !!! You must’ve had a hard journey out there in the cold, harsh world, but don’t you worry ! You’ve hit the right place ! Time flows differently here, my friend. No hurries, no worries, just take your time and explore the new world that you’ve entered. It’s a long way and we hope you have a wonderful experience as you let your mind flow, for Monobithi is nothing but ‘The Meanderings of Imagination’ !!!

You must be wondering now, what is Monobithi all about ? Hold on, we’ll give you the answer.

Monobithi is the place where imagination runs wild. It is the place where you can be who you are — not the one others know you to be, but the actual ‘you’ — the child in you, who wants a break from the world around, who wants to do the things that you love to do, who wants to have some real, some really real fun.

Still Confused?

Okay, let’s be more precise. Monobithi is an illustrated bilingual e-magazine,published twice a year , with a Spring and an Autumn issue . This magazine is for children, not ‘children’ by age, but the child that remains within all of us, like a tiny flicker of light. Here,we gather together to summon our creativity to the tip of our brush and the nib of our pen (and to all those other things which right now escapes my mind) and do whatever we feel like doing. We are lovers of art. Heard of the phrase ‘Art for art’s sake’? Well, that’s the motto we passionately believe in. And since you’re here, you must do so too ! That’s not all,though. Monobithi is our way of paying a small tribute to the man who taught us to live for art — Satyajit Ray, our teacher, guide and friend, whose ‘Sandesh’ is the biggest inspiration behind our magazine.

But hey ! We’ve been talking for quite a long time now. You should really start exploring now, for there’s a lot to explore ! Bon Voyage !!

The Editors

Aritra Bandyopadhyay

Aritra can be called a painter, sculptor, pianist and photographer. He is a passionate listener of Hindustani Classical music alongside being a published author [two Bengali fictions : 'Durga Pujoy Ashur Nidhon' (2017) and 'Lukochuri' (2018)]. He believes Satyajit Ray to be his teacher or ‘guru’ in all fields, and his motto in life is “Art for art’s sake”.

Education : Don Bosco School, Park Circus, Kolkata; 3rd Year BS-MS, Indian Institute of Science Education & Research (IISER), Mohali

Sumedha Ghosh Chowdhury

Sumedha is interested in painting, singing, elocution and whatever comes her way. An occasional author of poems and short stories, she is more into being a critic, an avid reader and analyzer. She, too, is an ardent follower of Ray, and believes that Art is the food that keeps her alive.

Education : Calcutta Girls High School, Kolkata; 2nd Year, BTech(EE), Jadavpur University, Kolkata

The Associates

Khushi Bhattacharya

Khushi is a painter and an excellent Bharatnatyam dancer. Eager to absorb innovations. She is a regular performer in college theaters and a dedicated follower of Satyajit Ray.

Education : Holy Child School, Jalpaiguri; 3rd Year BS-MS, Indian Institute of Science Education & Research (IISER), Mohali

Rajarshi Mukherjee

Rajarshi is the one who expresses what he feels through his sharp witted poetry. A photographer and a regular and extensive reader, Rajarshi is an ardent critic of the three masters of Bengali Cinema (Rittwik Ghatak, Mrinal Sen and Satyajit Ray).

Education : Don Bosco School, Park Circus, Kolkata; 3rd Year, BTech(ETCE), Jadavpur University, Kolkata
